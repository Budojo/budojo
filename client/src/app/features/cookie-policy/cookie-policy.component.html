Cookie Policy

What Budojo stores on your device, why, and how to change your mind. Aligned with the Italian Garante guidelines of 10 June 2021 and Article 5(3) of the e-Privacy directive.

Italiano · English

1. The short version

Budojo does not use tracking cookies, profiling cookies, or third-party advertising tags today. The only artefacts the SPA stores in your browser are strictly necessary for it to work — keeping you signed in, remembering a couple of UI preferences, and caching the app shell so it loads offline.

We still ship a consent banner because the moment we add anything non-essential — analytics, an embedded video, a marketing pixel — the law requires us to ask first. The banner is the gate; the categories below are what the gate covers.

2. Categories

The consent banner groups cookies and browser-side storage into four categories. Only the first one is locked on; the other three are opt-in.

  • Essential. Strictly necessary to deliver the service you asked for — keeping you signed in, the app shell cache, your language preference. Always on.
  • Preferences. Remembers small UI choices that personalise the experience but are not required for the service — e.g. the toggle that shows cancelled documents on the athlete detail.
  • Analytics. Anonymised usage telemetry we may add later to understand which features get used. None is wired today.
  • Marketing. Third-party pixels for retargeting or conversion tracking. None today and no plans on the immediate roadmap; ships as a gate so a future tool can be added cleanly.

3. What we actually store today

Pulled live from docs/legal/cookie-audit.md — the auditor-readable inventory in our repository.

Key Where Purpose Category
auth_token localStorage Sanctum bearer token so a refresh keeps you signed in. Essential
budojoLang localStorage Your saved language choice (English / Italian). Essential
budojoCookieConsent localStorage The very choice you make on the consent banner. Essential
documents.showCancelled localStorage Toggle remembering whether to show cancelled documents on the athlete detail. Preferences
Service worker cache Cache Storage Caches the JS / CSS / icons of the installed PWA. Essential

We do not set HTTP cookies. Authentication uses a bearer token (Sanctum) carried in the Authorization header — there is no session cookie.

4. Third parties

The current sub-processor list is at /sub-processors. None of them sets a cookie in your browser today on the Budojo origin: Cloudflare runs as a transparent proxy without bot cookies, DigitalOcean only sees server-side traffic, and Laravel Forge runs deploys not requests. Any change to that contract triggers an update to this page in the same release.

5. Change your mind

You can reopen the consent banner at any time and revise your choices.

Manage your preferences — or clear budojoCookieConsent from your browser's site data and the banner will show again on the next visit.

6. Re-consent on policy changes

The consent payload carries a version number. When we add a new category or change the meaning of an existing one substantively, we bump the version and the banner re-shows so you can renew your choice. The previous decision is not silently inherited.
