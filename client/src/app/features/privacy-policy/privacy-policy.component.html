Privacy Policy

Notice pursuant to Article 13 of EU Regulation 2016/679 (GDPR) for users of the Budojo service.

Italiano · English

Technical draft — under legal review. The final wording is being reviewed by counsel. The technical facts below (data controller, hosting region, retention windows, sub-processors, legal basis) are accurate and match what the production system actually does. Counsel may revise the wording, not the technical facts.

1. Data controller

Budojo — website budojo.it. Email for privacy requests: privacy@budojo.it (to be confirmed during legal review).

In many use cases the actual controller of the athletes' data is the academy that uses Budojo as its management software: in that case Budojo acts as a processor, under the terms of the DPA between Budojo and the academy. This notice covers instead the processing for which Budojo is the direct controller: the registration and management of the relationship with the user who opens the account.

2. Categories of data collected

Budojo only collects and processes the data necessary to operate the Service:

  • Identifying and contact data — first name, last name, email.
  • Credentials — bcrypt hash of the password, opaque Sanctum tokens (never plaintext).
  • Academy data — academy name, address, phone numbers, contact links.
  • Athlete data — first name, last name, date of birth, belt, registration status, guardian contacts for minors.
  • Attendance data — check-in dates of athletes at training sessions.
  • Uploaded documents — medical certificates, federation registrations, ID documents.
  • Technical metadata — IP address, user-agent, timestamps, limited to security and diagnostics.

Budojo does not collect geolocation data, advertising identifiers, or third-party tracking cookies.

3. Purposes and legal basis of processing

Purpose Legal basis (GDPR Art. 6)
Delivery of the Service requested by the user Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b))
Management of the contractual relationship, billing, service notifications Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b))
Tax and accounting obligations Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c))
Infrastructure security, abuse prevention, diagnostic logs Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f))

4. Retention period

  • Active user account — for the entire duration of the relationship.
  • Account scheduled for deletion — 30-day grace window during which the user can cancel the request. After this window the account is deleted irreversibly.
  • Billing and accounting data — 10 years from the date of issue (obligation under Art. 2220 of the Italian Civil Code).
  • Application logs — at most 12 months.

5. Processing methods and security measures

Data is processed with electronic tools and hosted in EU datacenters (Frankfurt — DigitalOcean fra1). Measures adopted:

  • Encrypted transmission (TLS 1.2+) for every request.
  • Passwords stored only as bcrypt hashes.
  • Bearer-token authentication (Laravel Sanctum); no session cookies.
  • Daily database backups with 30-day retention.
  • Administrative SSH access via key-based authentication.
  • Periodic audit of sub-processors.

Medical certificates, as health data (GDPR Art. 9), are subject to a separate assessment documented in docs/legal/dpia-medical-certificates.md.

6. Cookies and browser-side storage

Budojo does not use tracking cookies nor third-party profiling cookies.

  • HTTP cookies: none.
  • localStorage: four strictly technical keys — auth_token (Sanctum session token, strictly necessary to keep the user authenticated), budojoLang (selected interface language — `en` / `it`), budojoCookieConsent (the user's own consent record for non-essential categories, written by the cookie banner), and documents.showCancelled (UI preference for the documents filter). None identify or profile the user.
  • sessionStorage / IndexedDB: not used directly by the SPA.

The user-facing breakdown of categories, current keys, and how to revise consent lives at Cookie Policy. The full technical audit, with the verification methodology and the record of checks, is documented in docs/legal/cookie-audit.md within our source repository; a written request to privacy@budojo.it will return the current copy.

7. Recipients of the data — sub-processors

Data may be transmitted to the following parties as sub-processors. The canonical and up-to-date list is published at /sub-processors (English-only):

  • Cloudflare, Inc. — DNS, edge proxy / CDN, TLS termination.
  • DigitalOcean, LLC — VM hosting (EU region, Frankfurt).
  • Laravel Forge / Tighten Co. — provisioning and deployment automation.

Any change to this list is announced to the academy with 30 days' prior notice. Data is not transferred or sold to third parties for marketing or profiling purposes.

8. Transfers outside the EU

Personal data is stored within the EU (DigitalOcean Frankfurt). Cloudflare operates a global edge-node infrastructure: HTTP traffic transits through PoPs geographically close to the user, with transfer guarantees governed by the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (SCC).

9. Rights of the data subject

You may exercise the rights set out in Articles 15–22 GDPR at any time:

  • Right of access (Art. 15) — export via the "Profile" section (JSON + ZIP with documents).
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16) — edit directly from the "Profile" section.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17) — "Delete account" button in the "Profile" section; 30-day grace window.
  • Right to restriction (Art. 18) — email privacy@budojo.it.
  • Right to portability (Art. 20) — the export is in open JSON format.
  • Right to object (Art. 21) — email privacy@budojo.it.

10. Complaint to the supervisory authority

You have the right to file a complaint with the competent supervisory authority: for Italy the Garante per la protezione dei dati personali, at garanteprivacy.it.
